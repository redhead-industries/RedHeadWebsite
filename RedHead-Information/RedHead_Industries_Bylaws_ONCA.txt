Bylaws of RedHead Industries
(Ontario Not-for-Profit Corporations Act - ONCA)
ARTICLE 1 - GENERAL
1.1 Name
The name of the corporation is RedHead Industries (the "Corporation").

1.2 Jurisdiction
The Corporation is governed by the Ontario Not-for-Profit Corporations Act (ONCA).

1.3 Non-Profit Operation
The Corporation shall be carried on without the purpose of gain for its members. Any profits or other accretions shall be used solely to further the purposes of the Corporation.
ARTICLE 2 - PURPOSE
2.1 Purpose
The Corporation exists to carry out the purposes set out in its Articles of Incorporation, including supporting free and open-source software, privacy-respecting digital tools, community-driven technology initiatives, and open research activities for public benefit.
ARTICLE 3 - MEMBERS
3.1 Membership
The Corporation may have one or more members as determined by the Board of Directors.

3.2 Initial Member
The founder, Matthew DaLuz, shall be the initial and sole member of the Corporation unless additional members are admitted.

3.3 Admission of Members
Additional members may be admitted by resolution of the Board of Directors.

3.4 Rights of Members
Each member is entitled to one (1) vote at meetings of members.

3.5 Termination of Membership
Membership may be terminated by resignation or by resolution of the Board of Directors.
ARTICLE 4 - MEETINGS OF MEMBERS
4.1 Annual Meeting
An annual meeting of members shall be held at such time and place as determined by the Board.

4.2 Quorum
A quorum for meetings of members shall be a majority of the members entitled to vote.

4.3 Written Resolutions
Resolutions signed by all members entitled to vote are as valid as if passed at a meeting.
ARTICLE 5 - BOARD OF DIRECTORS
5.1 Authority
The affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by its Board of Directors.

5.2 Number of Directors
The Corporation shall have a minimum of one (1) director and a maximum of one (1) director.

5.3 Initial Director
The first and current director of the Corporation is Matthew DaLuz.

5.4 Term of Office
Directors shall hold office until they resign, are removed, or replaced.

5.5 Powers
The Board may appoint officers, admit members, approve expenditures, enter into contracts, and create or dissolve internal initiatives or branches.
ARTICLE 6 - OFFICERS
6.1 Officers
The Board may appoint officers including President, Secretary, and Treasurer. One person may hold multiple offices.

6.2 Duties
Officers shall perform duties assigned by the Board.
ARTICLE 7 - MEETINGS OF DIRECTORS
7.1 Meetings
Meetings of the Board may be held at any time and place determined by the Director.

7.2 Quorum
A quorum shall consist of a majority of directors.

7.3 Written Resolutions
A resolution signed by all directors is valid as if passed at a meeting.
ARTICLE 8 - CONFLICT OF INTEREST
8.1 Disclosure
Any director or officer with an interest in a contract or transaction shall disclose the interest.

8.2 Abstention
The interested director shall abstain from voting except as permitted by ONCA.
ARTICLE 9 - FINANCIAL MATTERS
9.1 Fiscal Year
The fiscal year shall be determined by the Board.

9.2 Banking
The Board shall designate financial institutions for the Corporation's funds.

9.3 Signing Authority
The Board may authorize officers or directors to sign instruments on behalf of the Corporation.
ARTICLE 10 - RECORDS
10.1 Records
The Corporation shall maintain Articles, bylaws, minutes, resolutions, financial records, and registers.
ARTICLE 11 - AMENDMENTS
11.1 Amendments
These bylaws may be amended by resolution of the Board and, where required, approval of members.
ARTICLE 12 - DISSOLUTION
12.1 Dissolution
Upon dissolution, remaining property shall be distributed in accordance with the Articles of Incorporation and ONCA.
