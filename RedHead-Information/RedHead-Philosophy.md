## Our Worldview

### 1. **Privacy Is Non-Negotiable**
Privacy is not a luxury. It is not a setting buried in a menu, a checkbox added for compliance, or a feature reserved for those who can afford it.
Privacy is a **basic human right**.

We reject, both in principle and in practice:
- Mass surveillance and bulk data collection
- Dark patterns designed to manipulate consent
- Exploitative data harvesting and resale
- Forced telemetry and opaque data pipelines
- Behavioral tracking disguised as ‚Äúanalytics‚Äù or ‚Äúoptimization‚Äù

Anything we build must respect user autonomy **by default**, not as an afterthought. If a system compromises privacy, it must be **explicitly opt-in, fully transparent, clearly documented, and easily reversible** ‚Äî or it does not ship.

We believe trust is earned through restraint, not extracted through design.

---

### 2. **Freedom Over Convenience**
Convenience is often used as an excuse to remove choice. We reject that trade-off.

We believe convenience should **never** come at the cost of freedom, agency, or understanding.

In practice, this means:
- Open formats over locked ecosystems
- Interoperability over vendor lock-in
- User control over platform control
- Clarity over abstraction that hides power dynamics

We design systems that **empower users**, even when doing so requires more effort, more documentation, or more responsibility on our part. Freedom may be harder ‚Äî but it is always worth it.

---

### 3. **Open Source Is a Moral Position**
Free and Open-Source Software (FOSS) is not merely a development methodology. It is a moral, social, and political stance.

We believe:
- Code should be auditable, inspectable, and understandable
- Knowledge should be shareable and preserved
- Innovation thrives through collaboration, not secrecy
- Trust should be earned through openness, not marketing

Whenever possible, **our work is released openly**, allowing anyone to study it, learn from it, modify it, redistribute it, or build upon it. Open source is how we ensure accountability ‚Äî including accountability to ourselves.

---

### 4. **Access Without Exploitation**
High-quality software, games, and research should not be locked behind exploitative systems, artificial scarcity, or manipulative business models.

We aim to:
- Build **high-quality, professional-grade software and experiences**
- Maintain high standards without predatory monetization
- Avoid manipulation, addiction-driven design, or artificial paywalls
- Ensure users are never the product

Innovation should elevate people, expand access, and reduce harm ‚Äî not extract value while giving as little back as possible.

---

### 5. **Transparency Creates Accountability**
Transparency is not about appearances ‚Äî it is about responsibility.

We believe:
- Decisions should be explainable
- Systems should be understandable
- Power should be visible

Where possible, our goals, methods, and limitations are communicated openly. Transparency allows communities to hold us accountable, challenge our decisions, and improve our work. Accountability is not a threat ‚Äî it is a safeguard.
